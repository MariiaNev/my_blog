Week 3:
Creating the Prototype

This week marked a major shift — from research and planning to hands-on prototyping. I began shaping the visual foundation of my concept, translating early ideas into something tangible and interactive.

Designing the Dashboard

Once that was done, I turned my attention to the main dashboard interface — the core of the user experience. I defined the menu structure and began designing the first set of dashboard components. I focused on the beginning stage of the home buying journey:

Step 1 – Preparing to Buy a House  (Thinking About Buying)

This stage includes tasks like exploring finances, understanding the market, and getting ready for the process ahead. I grouped the content into a logical structure, paying close attention to hierarchy, sequence, and user interaction — particularly how users will track their progress over time.

It took around 2–3 days to move from rough ideas to a working visual system. But this was a key moment — the project started to feel like a real product, not just a concept.



Lofi Prototype

Key Features:

Step Navigation:

At the top, users can see the full 5-step home-buying process. The current step is visually highlighted, giving a clear sense of progress and structure.


Progress Tracking:

A progress bar displays the estimated time to complete this step (4–6 weeks) and updates as tasks are marked complete.


To-Do List:

On the right side, a checklist helps users keep track of what’s ahead: watching an explainer video, reviewing property types, understanding costs, and more. It’s designed to be scannable, actionable, and motivating.


Explainer Video:

A short video introduces how the platform works, making the experience more accessible for first-time users. Alongside it, clear bullet points summarise the platform’s purpose and how to use it.


Supportive Content Blocks:

Below the video, users can explore more detailed content such as:

  • Property Types
  • Understanding the Costs
  • Deposit Options
