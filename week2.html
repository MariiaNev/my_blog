Week 2:
Deepening the Research

This week was dedicated to taking my UX research further — focusing on the complex, often overwhelming journey of buying a home in New Zealand. My goal was to better understand the needs, frustrations, and mental models of users navigating housing affordability.

Interview Planning

I drafted a set of interview questions and decided to split participants into two distinct groups: those who have already purchased property in NZ and those who are currently looking. This helped me approach the issue from both reflective and aspirational points of view.

Expanding the Secondary Research

I added more depth to my research by gathering additional data, reports, and user stories. These insights helped shape a clearer picture of the broader challenges.

Competitor & Market Analysis

I looked into existing tools and platforms related to property search and financing. The goal was to spot gaps and opportunities where thoughtful design could genuinely improve the experience.

Government Support

To understand the institutional landscape, I reviewed current support schemes for first-time buyers. This helped me identify both informational gaps and usability challenges in the way these programs are presented to the public.



Current State User Journey

Preparing for WIP 01

This week also included my first Work-In-Progress (WIP 01) presentation. It went well overall, but it became clear that my research still needed refinement — especially in terms of how insights were visually communicated.


Over the weekend, I focused on:

Empathy Mapping and Persona Development

Creating a detailed Current State User Journey


The journey mapping process took more time than I expected — the number of touchpoints, decisions, and emotional spikes throughout the housing process is huge. But unpacking that complexity was valuable. These tools are now guiding my thinking as I move into design ideation, helping me identify where targeted design interventions could make a real difference.
