Week 5:
Defining the Visual

This week was all about exploring the visual identity of the platform. After working through research, user journeys, and prototypes, it felt like the right time to start shaping the brand’s tone and look.

Building the Visual Direction

I began by collecting references and curating a moodboard to define the tone I wanted for the interface. My goal was to create a style that feels clear, friendly, and modern, while still maintaining a professional edge. The visual language I settled on is based on:

  • Simple colors
  • Clean lines
  • Minimal illustrations

This design approach prioritises clarity and ease of use. Despite its simplicity, the style carries a strong individual character by blending bold graphic elements with a soft, human-centered tone. The result is a platform that feels calm and supportive, while remaining visually distinct and easy to navigate.



Stylescape for the “Homely” Platform

Designing the Logo

Once the visual direction began to take shape, I moved on to designing a logo for the platform — something that would tie together the values of guidance, clarity, and forward momentum. Creating the logo helped me refine the overall aesthetic even further and led to some adjustments in dashboard design to ensure consistency.

The logomark represents movement, connection, and direction — aligning with the platform’s purpose: guiding users step by step through the home-buying journey with confidence.
