Week 6:
Hi-Fi Prototype

I continued working on the hi-fi prototype, focusing not only on visual polish but also on designing meaningful, user-focused features. The goal was to turn the concept into a functional and thoughtful experience.

Designing for Real Value

Instead of simply refining visuals, I focused on features that could genuinely support users on their home-buying journey — reducing friction, building clarity, and offering motivation.


Some of the elements I worked on included:

  • Progress indicators to track user momentum
  • Smart tooltips to explain tricky terms
  • Visual milestones as positive reinforcement
  • Collapsible sections to reduce cognitive load
  • Personalised guidance based on earlier input

Each decision was made with usability in mind: Does this help? Is it clear? Does it make progress feel achievable?

Learning from Competitor Analysis

I also revisited the competitor research I had conducted earlier. It revealed key gaps in other platforms — such as cluttered interfaces, lack of guidance, and assumptions that users already know what to do.


I used those insights to guide my own design process by focusing on:

  • A beginner-friendly, step-by-step experience
  • Timely, contextual support
  • Features that appear only when needed


Page of Homely platform - Smart Property Search

WIP 2 Presentation & Feedback

On Friday, I presented my design during WIP 2. The feedback was encouraging — the visual direction feels cohesive, and the user flow is coming together. The main point of critique, however, was about navigational complexity.

The structure should be slightly simplified to help users navigate more easily. It was also suggested that I conduct user testing to observe how intuitive the experience really is and whether users can confidently move through each step.

That said, I feel I’ve made meaningful progress. The platform is moving in the right direction — now it’s time to refine and validate.
