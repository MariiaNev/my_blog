Week 4:
Pitch & Design Strategy

This week was all about preparation — for both the pitch and the supporting design strategy document. I continued developing my lo-fi prototype, refining structure and layout to create a clearer.

Structuring the Story

Knowing my pitch would be on Friday, May 23 (the second day of presentations — our student order rotates randomly to keep things fair), I started crafting my Design Strategy document early. My goal was to shape a version that could also support the structure of my talk.

Most of the time was spent organizing the story I wanted to tell: what information to include, what to highlight, and how to walk my audience through the problem and solution with clarity. This strategy document became the backbone of my presentation.

Key Features:

  • A breakdown of New Zealand’s housing challenges, especially in Auckland.
  • Key takeaways from interviews with future and recent home buyers.
  • Barriers that stop people from acting — confusion, stress, and fear of making mistakes.
  • A defined problem space and target audience.
  • A detailed user journey map showing emotional shifts across each stage.
  • A clear solution: Homely, a step-by-step digital home buying assistant designed to reduce overwhelm and build user confidence.


Page 11 of the Design Strategy document

The presentation went well overall. We usually have 2–4 presentations each term, and with each one, I feel a little more at ease. That said, I still get nervous — especially because I know my English needs improvement — but with practice, I’m getting better at expressing my ideas clearly.
